Michael Jackson Trial Verdict Announcement

Jury delivers not guilty verdicts on all charges against Michael Jackson, ending the high-profile criminal trial.

Overview

On June 13, 2005, at approximately 2:20 PM Pacific Time, the jury in the Michael Jackson criminal trial delivered not guilty verdicts on all charges, bringing to a close one of the most closely watched celebrity trials in American legal history.

The Verdict Reading

Courtroom Atmosphere

The courtroom was packed with media, family members, and legal observers as Judge Rodney Melville prepared to read the verdicts. Jackson, wearing a white suit, sat with his legal team as the tension in the room was palpable.

Verdict Details

The jury found Jackson not guilty on all 10 counts:

  • Count 1: Conspiracy (Not Guilty)
  • Counts 2-5: Lewd acts upon a child (Not Guilty on all counts)
  • Counts 6-10: Administering alcohol to facilitate child molestation (Not Guilty on all counts)

Jackson's Reaction

Jackson remained composed during the reading of the verdicts, showing little visible emotion as each "not guilty" verdict was announced. His family members and supporters in the courtroom displayed more obvious relief and emotion.

Jury Deliberation Process

Timeline

The jury deliberated for approximately 32 hours over the course of seven days, indicating the serious consideration they gave to the evidence and testimony presented during the trial.

Jury Statements

Following the verdict, several jurors spoke to the media about their decision-making process. They cited credibility issues with key prosecution witnesses and insufficient evidence to prove guilt beyond a reasonable doubt.

Immediate Aftermath

Courtroom Response

The reading of the verdicts was met with subdued reactions in the courtroom, as Judge Melville had warned against any outbursts. Jackson's legal team showed visible relief, while the prosecution maintained professional composure despite the disappointing outcome.

Media Frenzy

Outside the courthouse, hundreds of media personnel and Jackson supporters had gathered. The announcement of the not guilty verdicts was met with cheers from Jackson fans who had maintained vigil throughout the trial.

Legal Analysis

Defense Success

Legal experts credited Jackson's defense team, led by Thomas Mesereau Jr., with mounting an effective defense that successfully challenged the prosecution's case and witness credibility.

Prosecution Challenges

The prosecution faced significant challenges throughout the trial, including credibility issues with key witnesses and what many legal observers saw as insufficient evidence to support the serious charges.

Public and Media Reaction

Immediate Coverage

News outlets around the world provided live coverage of the verdict announcement, with many noting the complete nature of Jackson's acquittal on all charges.

Celebrity Reactions

Numerous celebrities and entertainment industry figures issued statements supporting the verdict and expressing relief for Jackson and his family.

Historical Significance

Celebrity Justice

The verdict was seen by many as a significant moment in the intersection of celebrity, media, and the American justice system, demonstrating that even high-profile defendants could receive fair trials.

Impact on Jackson

While legally vindicated, the trial had taken a significant toll on Jackson's health, finances, and public image. The acquittal marked the end of the legal proceedings but not the end of the personal and professional challenges Jackson would face.
